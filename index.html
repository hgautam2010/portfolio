CERTIFIED BLOCKCHAIN UX DESIGNER

CBD

This program will guide the candidates through the concepts of blockchain technology, cryptoeconomics, and product UX design. By completing the program, participants will be able to understand the theoretical underpinnings of how to apply design-thinking, systems-thinking, and exponential-thinking into the design of blockchain (distributed/decentralized) solutions. In addition, participants will have a deep and wide understanding of the social impact this technology can have. Participants will get to review UX case-studies within the blockchain space, learn more about existing solutions, and receive guidance from industry experts on how to design an effective blockchain solution. There will be a strong focus on Ethereum.

This program consists of three days intensive training course and one week of group work on a capstone project. You will receive a certificate upon completion of the course.

Prerequisite:

  • CBP Certification or Profound knowledge of blockchain technology

Course Modules

This program consists of three days intensive training course and one week of group work on a capstone project. You will receive a certificate upon completion of the course.

Module1-Intensive Training Module (3 days)

DAY 1 – HIGH-LEVEL

9:00 to 10:00am: What blockchain and design is! This will be a quick recap of how blockchain and product design relates to each other. Blockchain is first and foremost a design principle. What are the affordances with regards to blockchain?

10:00 to 11:00am: Innovation-Thinking – a combination of design-thinking + exponential-thinking + systems-thinking

11:00 to 12:00pm: How Cryptoeconomics and Tokenization relates to UX Design?

1:00 to 3:00pm: Discussion on blockchain protocols such as consensus and oracles; and how this relates to design.Discussion on behavioural economics and how this relates to blockchain

3:00 to 5:00pm: Circular Economies and Circular Design as well as Social Innovation/Impact

DAY 2 – MID-LEVEL

9:00 to 10:00am: Decentralized Governance and how does this relates to design

10:00 to 11:00am: Proof of X – what all these “Proof of ___” mean? And how does this relate to design

11:00am to 12pm: Discussion on additional blockchain concepts such as Escrow, Public vs. Private vs. Permissioned blockchains, gas, etc. and how it relates to product design as well as business design. The ultimate goal of this section is to talk about what the potential new business model of the Web 3.0 world will be. Cloud computing gave birth to Software-as-a- Service as well as IaaS and PaaS. But now, with distributed computing and decentralized applications what will be the new business model?

1:00 to 3:00pm: Gamification + Blockchain + UX Design

3:00 to 5:00pm: Blockchain and Data Visualization

DAY 3 – UX CASE-STUDY

9:00 to 10:00am: Case-Study #0: lightning discussions on all the existing applications of blockchain which tackle low-hanging fruit problems.

10:00 to 12:00pm: Case-Study #1: real-world example (higher-hanging fruit)

1:00 to 3:00pm: Case-Study #2: personal project

3:00 to 5:00pm: Case-Study #3: speculative design

INTRODUCTION:

Bitcoin is the first blockchain-based decentralized digital currency that is progressively being utilized everywhere worldwide since 2009. It has advanced its development into many other assets and forms of Blockchain technology.

The industry has been developing more rapidly than the boldest forecasts predicted. Capitalization of the crypto-currency market has approached the $ 100 billion mark in 2017.Over the last decade, the world of digital currencies underwent the major transformation with the introduction of Bitcoins in the market. Bitcoin is a decentralized cryptocurrency, purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash used for online transactions through digital unit of exchange, where a user can make transaction without any middlemen.

Transactions made are processed through private network of computers, linked through shared program and simultaneously it gets verified and recorded in public distributed ledger, called the block chain. Bitcoin is the most prominent triple entry bookkeeping system in existence. Global Bitcoin Technology Market - Market Trends and Forecasts (2015 - 2020)

Blockchain technology, which is best known for powering Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies, is gaining steam among finance firms because of its potential to streamline processes and increase efficiency. The technology could cut costs by up to $20 billion annually by 2022.

That's because blockchain, which operates as a distributed ledger, has the ability to allow multiple parties to transfer and store sensitive information in a space that’s secure, permanent, anonymous, and easily accessible. That could simplify paper-heavy, expensive, or logistically complicated financial systems, like remittances and cross-border transfer, shareholder management and ownership exchange, and securities trading, to name a few. And outside of finance, governments and the music industry are investigating the technology’s potential to simplify record-keeping.

As a result, venture capital firms and financial institutions alike are pouring investment into finding, developing, and testing blockchain use cases. Over 50 major financial institutions are involved with collaborative blockchain startups, have begun researching the technology in-house, or have helped fund startups with products rooted in blockchain.

In BI Intelligence’s Blockchain Report, we explain how blockchain works, why it has the potential to provide a watershed moment for the financial industry, and the different ways it could be put into practice in the coming years.

Keynotes:
  • Spending on capital markets applications of blockchain is expected to grow at a 52% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) through 2019, according to Aite Group, to reach $400 million that year.
  • Banks and major financial institutions are working both collaboratively and independently to develop blockchain tech. Over 50 major financial institutions are involved with collaborative blockchain startups, like R3 CEV or Chain. And many are investing in the technology on their own as well.
  • Putting blockchain to use for real-world transactions is likely not that far off. If working groups' tests are successful, firms could be using it to transact real value as early as the end of this year and we could see widespread industry application within the next few years.

Bitcoin is a decentralized cryptocurrency, purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash used for online transactions through digital unit of exchange, where a user can make transaction without any middlemen. Transactions made are processed through private network of computers, linked through shared program and simultaneously it gets verified and recorded in public distributed ledger, called the book chain. Bitcoin is the most prominent triple entry bookkeeping system in existence. Some of the key benefits of using bitcoin are: there are no intermediaries or third party involved in, to interrupt, lesser transaction fees, multimode payments methods, daily cash outs and others.

Bitcoin, have paved the way for increased mainstream acceptance for the digital currency, which even has the potential for transforming global financial transactions. In current scenario, most of the online merchants and startup companies have already started enjoying the anonymity and efficiency of bitcoin payments, which allows them to cut overhead costs compared to credit or debit cards transactions.

The Global Bitcoin Technology Market is estimated to grow at a CAGR of 7.26% over the period 2015 - 2020. Booming economy and growing disposable income among middle income group in developing world has led Bitcoin technologies to gain traction and to be used as one of the medium of exchange in the region.

However, the recent regulations and directives imposed by the European banking authorities has hugely impacted the Bitcoin market in Europe. Whereas, owing to large number of bitcoin users, the market will witness growth in the North America region.

Global Bitcoin technology market have been broadly segmented on the basis of denominations used, services offered, industry verticals and geography. Whereas per services it has been segmented by bitcoin exchanges, remittance services, and payment & wallet services. Various industry verticals, where bitcoin are being used are mining, banking and financial services, entertainment and media.

Emerge

An Introduction: Emerge

Leapfrogging is when a community or organization uses emerging technologies to bypass steps in traditional development cycles. This jump often leads to economic growth, increased productivity, and an overall drastic improvement of quality of life.

Enter blockchain — a technological breakthrough that is accelerating the transformation of political, social and economic realities in emerging market countries. And while the technology is nascent, the opportunities are seemingly endless. Blockchain is both inevitable and unknown, which is also why it is now our collective responsibility to ensure we use its powers for social good, and that we develop the technology and its derivatives in a more inclusive and more responsible manner than any technology before it.

Resilient blockchain technologies can increase access to financial services and resources, enable fair judicial processes and transparent voting mechanisms, and efficiently distribute energy or healthcare services. Blockchain for social good is transformative and it has inspired a whole new class of entrepreneurs to emerge.

We are Emerge, a social enterprise that leverages blockchain to create socio-economic inclusion for emerging market and diaspora communities around the world. Our team is made up of diaspora entrepreneurs familiar with the struggles of building businesses in our home and adopted countries. We are therefore committed to using blockchain to inspire and facilitate grassroots innovation.

Here are some of the projects we have been working on.

From Our Product Studio

Raise, a diaspora direct investment platform that leverages smart contracts, milestones, social impact metrics and token incentives to enable diaspora community members to invest in emerging market entrepreneurs. Raise aims to redefine traditional investment by creating an international economic bridge that leads to strong local startup ecosystems in regions such as Latin America, the Caribbean, Africa, the Middle East and Asia. Our product placed second at a major international blockchain hackathon and will be ready to launch in 2018.

Homeward, a blockchain enabled platform to expedite and improve the country referral process for refugee resettlement. Homeward aims to bring the humanity back to the way we process refugee applications, and ultimately lead refugees to the right place to rebuild their homes and lives. Our product is in the early stages of development and currently in progress at one of the world’s largest international blockchain hackathons.

Research

Earlier this year, we introduced Leap, the first research publication to track blockchain projects in emerging markets. We provided data on 448 projects across Latin America, the Middle East, Africa, Asia, the Caribbean and Eastern Europe, highlighting exemplary use cases and regional industries of interest. Leap 2.0 will be released in 2018, featuring more and deeper data. Please email us at hello@emergedev.co if you are interested in collaborating on this project.

Education

e:disrupt, a series of blockchain for social impact hackathons. Our first event will be in Toronto, Canada, on May 31, 2018 in partnership with the MaRS Discovery District. Our second will be held in Hong Kong during the summer of 2018.

Belt — “Your Financial Utility Belt”

A Case-Study

Who

Product Manager = Felix G.

Interaction Designer = Tian Z.

Software Engineer = Alex T.

AI Engineer = Agata B.

Why

Team Reasons — threefold

  1. Tian has some subject matter expertise in this realm and is incredibly passionate about blockchain/cryptoassets
  2. After much debate about what problem space to tackle based on the Felix’s initial pitched problem space of “using AI to improve HR”, we decided to tackle a less competitive realm.
  3. Agata has specific interest in Natural Language Processing and we found a better application of her skills in the blockchain/cryptoasset problem space vs. HR.

User Problem — threefold, users want to invest in cryptoassets but

  1. have a hard time to invest in cryptoassets
  2. have little time to invest in cryptoassets
  3. have a difficult time trusting in certain cryptoassets

Market Opportunity — threefold, blockchain may be more valuable of a technology but cryptoassets

  1. can be securities (re: security tokens) and therefore have potential for great gains
  2. can be utilities (re: utility tokens) and therefore act as an interface to blockchain
  3. represent the future of capitalism (re: Capitalism 2.0)
It’s discussed here in Belt’s first published article

What

Belt is a fintech app which allows you to seamlessly make microinvestments into Bitcoin, Ether, or Litecoin (for now) with your spare change (round-up, top-up, or percentage of every transaction you make). We believe that cryptoassets and the blockchain technology represent not only the next evolutionary step of the internet but also capitalism overall.

Why we’re called “Belt”?

  1. Inspired by other successful fintech companies such as Stripe and Plaid.
  2. Belts are adjustable, configurable, and possess freedom of control, therefore brings about accessibility
  3. Belts fastens, combines, and integrates things together, therefore brings about efficiency
  4. Belts are invisible, seamless, and genderless, therefore is universal
  5. And finally of course, we’re your Financial Utility “Belt”

I elaborate more on our philosophy here — https://medium.com/usebelt/introducing-belt-your-financial-utility-belt-3464fddf1b7f

But in short, our 3 core values are:

  1. Efficiency
  2. Utility
  3. Accessibility

How we Tackled all 3 Problems:

  1. Hard Time — by investing spare change, a smaller risk is taken by the user
  2. Little Time — by simply connecting your bank and making purchases, not much time needs to be spent in order to make investments
  3. Belt Score — by providing this, we enable calmness, clarity, and context for the user to aid in their decision-making process.

Where — it could still go

  1. Inclusion of ICO’s or TGE’s
  2. Inclusion of B2B model: employee wages
  3. Inclusion of algo-trading

Content:

Problem:

The client’s request, after being processed by Hivewire’s CEO was for a crowdfunding platform that would allow not only property developers to raise funds in a crowdsourced manner but property owners to do the same for lease or ownership. This would be an end-to-end platform as it would cover all stages of a real-estate project from 0 to 1. This product would end up being a white-label that could be used by any/all real-estate companies such as Re/Max. The name “Re/Crowd” is an unofficial name given by myself for the intent of clarity.
